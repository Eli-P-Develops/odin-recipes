Country Fried Steak

Background of Country Fried Steak

Country fried steak AKA chicken fried or pan fried steak is a classic southern homestyle dish. It is thought to have originated in Southeastern US in the early 1800s, an idea brought over from a traditionally German meal. Country fried steak is most often served with the typical southern fixin's and is well known as a popular soul food in the south.

Ingredients

  • 4, 8 oz cube steaks
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 ¾ cups whole milk
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ cup heavy cream

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 225˚F for 20 minutes
  2. Place a steak in the middle of a cutting board, then wrap it in plastic wrap. Pound the steak evenly to a thickness of 1/4 inch with a meat mallet. Season with salt and pepper on both sides. Repeat this process for each steak
  3. Whisk together the eggs and 1 cup of milk in a large, shallow bowl. Mix the 1 1/2 cups of flour, pepper, salt, onion powder, garlic powder, and paprika in a separate shallow bowl
  4. The steaks should be first dredged in the flour mixture, then dip into the egg mixture and allow any extra egg to drip off. Repeatedly coat with the flour mixture. Wait 10-15 minutes for the coating to dry out
  5. Heat the oil in a 10-inch pan over medium-high heat until it reaches 375˚F
  6. Fry the steaks, 2 at a time, for 3 minutes, until golden brown and crispy. Flip and cook on the other side for 3 minutes more. The steaks should be golden brown and cooked through. Check internal temperature for around 155˚-165˚F, then transfer the steaks to a paper towel-lined plate or baking sheet and season with salt. Once all of the steaks are done frying, transfer to the oven and begin preparing the gravy
  7. Pour the hot oil into a heatproof bowl and let cool before discarding. 9. Leave any browned bits in the pan
  8. Before discarding, pour the boiling oil into a heatproof bowl and allow it to cool. Do not remove any browned bits from the pan.
  9. Melt the butter at medium heat. Whisk 3 more tablespoons of flour together. Cook the roux for 2-3 minutes, or until it turns light brown. Add the remaining milk and heavy cream. Stirring periodically, bring to a simmer, and cook for 5-7 minutes until thickened and add salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Serve the steaks with gravy and your preferred side dishes.
  11. Enjoy!
